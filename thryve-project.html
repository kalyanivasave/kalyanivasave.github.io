Thryve: K-12 Classroom Readiness Platform

A classroom readiness platform that gives teachers real-time visibility into student states and enables them to provide targeted, evidence-backed support without disrupting instruction.

Problem

Students often start class with varying levels of readiness—some carry over stress or distraction, others are cognitively overloaded. Teachers notice these patterns but lack real-time visibility into readiness states and structured support options that don't disrupt instruction. As a result, learning often begins on schedule rather than when students are actually ready.

Solution

Built a K-12 platform that provides real-time readiness signals and allows teachers to deliver targeted support in the moment. The platform includes:

  • Student readiness check-ins that are quick and age-appropriate
  • Teacher dashboard showing class readiness overview
  • Evidence-based micro-interventions (focus reset, grounding, clarity support, reflection prompts)
  • SEL module library aligned with CASEL and TEKS
  • Role-specific experiences for teachers, students, parents, and administrators
  • Skill tracking across emotional regulation, social awareness, and decision-making

Key Accomplishments

  • Multi-stakeholder design: separate, tailored experiences for teachers, students, parents, and school administrators
  • Age-adaptive interfaces for K-5, 6-8, and 9-12 grade bands
  • CASEL and TEKS alignment ensuring standards-based content
  • Privacy-first architecture with role-based visibility controls
  • Evidence-informed intervention architecture mapped to learning science research
  • Professional iconography and consistent design system

Frameworks & Compliance

  • CASEL Aligned: Supports development of self-awareness, self-management, social awareness, relationship skills, and responsible decision-making
  • TEKS Compatible: Aligned with Texas Essential Knowledge and Skills standards
  • Evidence-Based: Grounded in cognitive load theory and learning science research

Note: Thryve is a support tool for classroom readiness, not a diagnostic or clinical instrument.
