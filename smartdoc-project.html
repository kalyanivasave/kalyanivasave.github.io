Cognitive Overload

Critical patient information is scattered across multiple EHR tabs, forcing physicians to mentally piece together data during precious patient interaction time.

Burnout Factor

Poor EHR design contributes to 40% of physician burnout, directly impacting healthcare delivery and professional satisfaction.

Research & Validation

Evidence-Based Foundation

Academic Research

Comprehensive review of published findings from JAMA, Stanford Medicine, NIH, and JMIR to understand the scope and impact of EHR usability issues.

Live EHR Analysis

Studied live EHR interfaces, specifically PrognoCIS, to map friction points and identify specific usability challenges physicians face daily.

Key Findings

  • Poor EHR design is a key contributor to 40% of physician burnout
  • Fragmented information architecture reduces patient interaction time
  • Tab-switching creates cognitive load and increases error potential
  • Pre-visit preparation is inefficient and time-consuming
Solution

Smart Pre-Visit Summary Tool

Hypothesis

Providing doctors with a smart, rule-driven pre-visit summary will reduce prep time by 25%, improve quality of care, and lower EHR fatigue.

Core Features

Floating Summary Panel

Consolidates critical patient data in a single, scan-optimized view that doesn't require tab-switching.

Rule-Based Logic

Intelligent grouping of red-flag conditions, medication interactions, and chronic conditions using predefined clinical rules.

Visit Preparation

Aggregates visit reason, current vitals, medication history, and allergies from multiple data sources.

Scan-Read Optimization

Layout specifically designed for rapid information consumption during the critical pre-visit window.
